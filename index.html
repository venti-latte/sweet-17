Dear Dani,

I know I hurt you. I know I made you feel unseen on your birthday, and I know saying "I'm sorry" doesn't automatically undo that. But I want to explain everything.

I had everything ready for you. I had a bouquet of lilies with a QR code attached that led to a website I made just for you. This website. I had it all planned. I was so excited to give it to you.

When I came home Thursday night, my room had been gone through. My mom went through the drawer where I keep everything you've ever given me and she took all of it. Including the bouquet. She had it thrown out.

I got angry. My dad hit me for it. My arms got bruised. That's why I didn't go to school on Friday.

I'm telling you this because I can't let you believe, even for a second, that I forgot about you. That I didn't try. That you weren't worth showing up for. You said you felt unloved. I hate that what happened at home took that from us. I don't know what we figure out from here.

But I didn't want another day to pass with you thinking I didn't care. I care. More than I know how to say. I'm sorry I couldn't tell you this sooner. I didn't want you to worry.

Please don't ever doubt that I love you. I would give the world to you if I could. You know that. I'm sorry I just couldn't on your special day. You are the person I think about when something good happens. You are the reason I save things in drawers. I love you, Dani. More than I know how to say out loud.

Yours always,

— Joe
